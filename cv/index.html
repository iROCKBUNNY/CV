Summary

Brief Biography

Tong Qu is a research and development engineer at State Grid Smart Grid Research Institute working on power quality. He received the master's degree in electronic and information engineering from the Hong Kong Polytechnic University in 2013, and the bachelor's degree in communication engineering from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications in 2012. He was a visiting scholar at Multimedia Computing Lab, Renmin University of China, studying web image retrieval. His postgraduate research at PolyU is focused on human face recognition with low-resolution face images. His research interests include computer vision, pattern classification and recognition, object detection and tracking, signal and image processing, and machine learning.

Publications

Master Thesis

  • Title: Low-Resolution Face Recognition
  • Supervisor: Prof. Kenneth K. M. Lam
  • Description: Traditional face recognition algorithms are not best suited for matching face images captured at a low resolution to a set of high-resolution gallery face images. To perform matching between face images of different resolutions, this work proposes a coupled-projection method. Based on local relationships in the data, two sets of projections are learned: one for low-resolution face images and the other for high-resolution face images, respectively. Then, both low-resolution probe face images and high-resolution gallery face images are projected into a common subspace. Hence, subsequent matching can be done in the subspace directly.
